  • About The Author

    • 24 Sep 2021
    • 2 min read

    • By the day I attend college classes and try not to doze off in between. I brew a cup of coffee, plan my day after college and get going. It's a battle against bad habits and distractions every day. By evening I'm on the ground to play football, run, hit the gym sweat and breathe in the fresh air. And at night, that's when the stories are made. I read I write, I learn things in any and every way possible.

    And on the weekends I share them here with you.

    Hi, I'm Swastik Shrey, an electronics engineering student at NIT Karnataka, India. I'm a huge fan of self-education, and I feel it's the only way for an ordinary person to become extraordinary.

    My learning always went beyond schooling which is why I was never the best at school, but my love and curiosity for learning things and skills along with practical experiences exceeds the rest. A few things that I love to do and I'm good at are:

    1. Electronics/Electrical 🔌
    2. Football ⚽
    3. Guitar 🎸
    4. Reading and self-learning in general 📚
    5. ✨Magic✨ (that's right!)
    6. Documenting and organising things in life.  
    7. Content creation - Writing, Designing etc

    And puns yeah, my friends hate me for it but I like to believe I'm good. I also code, swim, speed type, create content, teach and watch A TON of YouTube (gotta be my guilty pleasure).

    I love being good at things I do, following a discipline and helping others get good at it too. That's the gist of this whole blog. I also like underdogs, meaningful movies and songs, and love my friends and family.

    Most importantly, I like standing out from the crowd. Hence the name, dare to differ. If I had to define my values in 5 words I'd say courage, consistency, gratitude, self-education and COMO (celebrate on missing out).

    Stayed in a lot of cities in India like Kolkata, Patna, Chennai, Pune, Mumbai and Mangalore. Mostly Pune (and I LOVE it!). I've been a good student but not a fan of classes so bunked hundreds of times (and had many trips to the Principal's office 😬).

    I love Karna from Mahabharata (search his story already if you haven't) and I read the Mahabharata just to know what did he wrong, why was he punished to death. Lord Krishna says he didn't use his innate talent of archery and his power to uplift others like him, instead he sought vengeance and external approval. It hit me. The World would be such a better place if we could find just one younger person like ourselves and help them, guide them and give them what we always wanted but never had.

    Okay Swastik why the boring story?
    Well, that's what makes me tick. I don't know about changing the world someday but I'd like to change A world someday, someone's world I can relate to. This blog is a small step in that direction.

    That's a lot about me now I want to know more about you! Be sure to get in touch with me via any platform, all contact links are provided on the contacts page.
